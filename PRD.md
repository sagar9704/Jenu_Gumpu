# Project Title: 37 – Android App Development using GenAI: Jenu-Gumpu (Agriculture/Self-Employment)

## 1. Project Selection
I have selected **Project Title: 37 – Android App Development using GenAI: Jenu-Gumpu (Agriculture/Self-Employment)** as my individual project for the MindMatrix VTU Internship Program. This project focuses on empowering tribal and rural honey hunters by providing them with a digital platform to manage their harvest, improve product quality through grading, and achieve better market value through a collective bargaining model.

## 2. Problem Statement
Tribal and rural honey hunters often sell raw honey to middlemen at very low prices. They lack the knowledge of "Value Add" (filtering, grading, branding) and are often unaware of the actual retail market prices in urban centers. This information asymmetry results in minimal profits for the actual producers while middlemen capture most of the value. There is also a lack of traceability and standardized grading at the source.

## 3. Detailed Description (The Vision)
Jenu-Gumpu acts as a **"Honey Producer’s Collective"** application. It is designed to transform the honey hunter into a brand owner. The application provides a "Honey Grading Guide" (color and moisture checks), tracks the floral source (e.g., Coffee blossom, Wildflower), and allows the group to log their "Total Collective Stock" to negotiate better prices with big retailers. By digitizing the harvest and grading process, it builds trust and transparency for both the producers and the consumers.

## 4. App Usage & User Flow
*   **Harvest Log**: Record the date, location, and quantity of honey collected to maintain a digital history of production.
*   **Grading Tool**: A visual guide to check honey quality, including a simulated moisture test to ensure the product meets market standards.
*   **Price Monitor**: Real-time comparison between retail market prices and wholesale prices, helping users understand their bargaining power.
*   **Batch Tracker**: Assigns a unique "Batch ID" to honey jars for traceability from the forest to the final consumer.
*   **Profit Calculator**: A logic-based tool that calculates estimated earnings after accounting for filtering and other processing costs.

## 5. Technical Implementation & Hints
*   **Frontend (UI)**:
    *   Designed using **XML Layout Design**.
    *   Use of **CardViews** for different honey grades and batch entries.
    *   **RecyclerView** for displaying harvest history.
    *   **Kannada Language Support**: The UI will be available in Kannada to ensure accessibility for tribal and rural users.
    *   Use of **Icons** for grading and navigation to make the app intuitive.
*   **Backend (Data Management)**:
    *   **Room Database**: Used for storing harvest history, batch data, and local user records.
    *   **Shared Preferences**: For storing user settings and basic profile info.
*   **Development Tools**:
    *   Android Studio
    *   Java (Recommended)
    *   Room DB for persistence

## 6. Impact Goals
*   **Tribal Empowerment**: Improving the livelihoods and economic independence of forest-dwelling communities.
*   **Organic Growth**: Promoting high-quality, "Forest-to-Table" chemical-free products that command a premium.
*   **Sustainable Harvest**: Providing guidelines on how to harvest honey sustainably without killing the bee colony, ensuring ecological preservation.

## 7. Success Criteria for Students
*   The app successfully allows users to categorize honey by **"Floral Source"**.
*   The **"Collective Stock"** feature is viewable as a sum of all individual entries, enabling collective bargaining.
*   The UI is functional in **Kannada** and uses intuitive icons for the grading process.
*   The **Profit Calculator** accurately estimates earnings based on user input.
*   The application maintains a clean, professional, and accessible design.

## 8. Expected Outcome
The final Android application will provide a strong platform for honey hunters to manage their production and improve their income. It will demonstrate practical Android development skills including local database management with Room, UI localization, and the implementation of business logic for profit calculation and stock management.

## 9. Conclusion
Jenu-Gumpu is not just an app—it is a tool for economic liberation for tribal honey hunters. By combining technology, quality standards, and collective effort, it solves a meaningful real-world problem in the agriculture and self-employment sector. It is practical, achievable, and addresses critical issues of transparency and fair trade.
